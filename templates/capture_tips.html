üí° Lighting Conditions
  • Use natural, even lighting when possible
  • Avoid harsh shadows on face
  • Not too bright (no overexposure)
  • Not too dark (face should be clearly visible)
  • Capture in different lighting if you'll use the system in varied conditions
üé≠ Facial Expressions
  • Include both neutral and smiling expressions
  • Keep eyes open and looking at camera
  • Avoid extreme expressions (very wide mouth, squinting)
  • Natural, relaxed face works best
üìè Distance & Positioning
  • Face should fill 60-80% of the frame
  • Not too close (image may blur)
  • Not too far (lose facial details)
  • Keep face centered in frame
  • Ensure entire face is visible
üö´ Things to Avoid
  • Motion blur (stay still when capturing)
  • Reflections from glasses (tilt slightly if needed)
  • Heavy makeup that changes facial features
  • Hats or items covering forehead/face
  • Backlighting (light source behind you)
üîÑ After Capturing
  • Train the model immediately after adding new faces
  • Test recognition from different angles
  • Add more photos if recognition fails
  • Aim for 10-15 photos per person for best results
  • Retrain after adding significant new samples
